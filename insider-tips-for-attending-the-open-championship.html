Insider Tips for Attending The Open Championship

The Open Championship is as much a cultural event as it is a sporting contest, and a little preparation goes a long way to enjoying it fully.

1. Prepare for All Weather: Even in July, conditions can swing from warm sunshine to cold wind and rain. Bring layers, waterproof gear, and a sturdy pair of shoes for walking the course.

2. Arrive Early: Morning tee times often feature fewer crowds, giving you better views and easier movement between holes.

3. Move with the Action: Unlike stadium sports, golf allows you to follow your favourite players across the course. Plan your route to see key holes like Royal Birkdale’s famous par 3s and closing stretch.

4. Explore the Village: The Open’s spectator village has big screens, food stalls, and merchandise stands — perfect for a mid-round break.

5. Local Accommodation Matters:Staying near the host course makes early starts and late finishes easier. Sport Air packages secure prime locations to cut down on travel.

By combining the drama of links golf with the atmosphere of the UK’s coastal towns, The Open is a truly unique experience.
